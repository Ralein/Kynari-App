## App Store Description

**Understand your toddler's emotional world — without recording a single word.**

Kynari is a privacy-first emotion detection app that listens for emotional tone in your child's voice. It doesn't record words. It doesn't store audio. It uses on-device AI to detect how your child feels — happy, sad, frustrated, or calm — and shows you the patterns over time.

**How it works:**
1. Place your phone in the room during playtime
2. Kynari's AI listens for emotional tone (not words)
3. You get a daily emotion report on your dashboard

**Key Features:**
• 🧠 On-device AI — All processing happens on your phone. Zero audio leaves the device.
• 📊 Daily emotion reports — See your child's emotional patterns at a glance
• 📈 Weekly AI narratives — Warm, personalized weekly summaries powered by AI
• 🔔 Smart alerts — Get notified about extended frustration or unusual quiet
• 📅 14-day baseline — Kynari learns your child's "normal" and flags deviations
• 👨‍👩‍👧 Multi-child support — Track multiple children from one account

**Privacy you can trust:**
• Audio is NEVER recorded, stored, or transmitted
• Only emotion labels (happy, sad, etc.) reach our servers
• COPPA compliant — built for children's privacy from day one
• Full data export and deletion at any time

**For parents who want to understand, not surveil.**

Kynari helps you see emotional patterns you might miss — like post-nap crankiness every Tuesday or increasing joy during creative play. It's like a smart baby monitor for feelings.

Designed for toddlers ages 1–5. Start your 7-day calibration today.

## App Review Notes

Kynari uses the microphone to detect emotional tone (prosody) in a toddler's voice. It does NOT perform speech recognition or record any audio.

How to test:
1. Create an account with any email
2. Add a child profile (name + date of birth)
3. Tap "Start Monitoring" — the app listens for 3-second windows
4. After a few minutes, check the dashboard for emotion events
5. The weekly AI report generates after 7 days of data

The microphone is used exclusively for on-device emotion detection. Audio buffers are discarded immediately after inference (within milliseconds). No audio is ever transmitted to our servers.
